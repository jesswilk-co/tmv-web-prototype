End of Life Care

When the time comes, we'll be there.

Saying goodbye is never easy. We believe your pet deserves to be at home, surrounded by the people who love them, in the place they feel safest.

Tony's approach

Home is where this should happen.

After years of supporting families through this moment, Tony is certain of one thing: a calm, familiar environment makes all the difference — for your pet and for you.

No clinical setting.

Your pet stays on their favourite bed, in their favourite room. There are no bright lights, no strangers, no cold examination tables. Just the warmth of home.

No rush.

We book extended appointments so there is never any pressure on time. You set the pace. If you need another five minutes, or another hour, that's okay.

The whole family can be there.

Children, partners, other pets — everyone who wants to say goodbye can be present. We gently explain each step so everyone understands what's happening.

"This is the hardest call we take, and the most important visit we make."

Tony sees end-of-life care not as the end of a relationship, but as the last act of love you give your pet. Everything we do is designed to make this moment as peaceful and dignified as possible.

If you're unsure whether it's the right time, we're happy to talk it through — no pressure, no judgement. Sometimes a conversation is all you need.

What to expect

A gentle, unhurried process.

We want you to know exactly what will happen, so there are no surprises. Every step is explained beforehand, and you're in control throughout.

1

The conversation.

Before the visit, we'll talk through your pet's condition, answer your questions, and discuss what you'd like to happen. We'll explain all the options, including sedation and aftercare, so you can make decisions at your own pace.

2

Sedation.

We strongly recommend sedation before the procedure. A gentle injection helps your pet become deeply relaxed and sleepy, usually within a few minutes. This means they feel no anxiety or discomfort. You can hold them, stroke them, and be close.

3

The procedure.

Once your pet is peacefully sedated, a final injection is given. It is quick, painless, and peaceful. Your pet simply falls into a deeper sleep and their heart gently stops. We stay quietly with you for as long as you need.

4

Aftercare.

We'll take care of everything afterwards. You can choose communal or private cremation, and we offer keepsakes like a clay paw print or fur clipping. We handle all arrangements so you don't have to worry about logistics during a difficult time.

Aftercare

Remembering your pet.

How you choose to remember your pet is deeply personal. We offer a range of options and we'll guide you through each one without any pressure.

Communal cremation.

Your pet is cremated alongside other animals in a respectful, shared ceremony. Ashes are scattered in a dedicated garden of remembrance. This is often chosen by families who find comfort in the idea of their pet resting in a peaceful, natural setting.

Private cremation.

Your pet is cremated individually and their ashes are returned to you in a chosen container. This usually takes 7–14 days. Many families find comfort in keeping their pet's ashes at home or scattering them in a meaningful place.

Keepsakes.

A clay paw print in a frame, a fur clipping in a keepsake bottle, or additional cremation wishes — small, tangible ways to hold onto your pet's memory. These are prepared with care during the visit itself.
