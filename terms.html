Part A

General Terms & Conditions

1. Our Services

Veterinary Services: We provide veterinary care. We offer mobile consultations in the comfort of your own home, as well as virtual consultations. Our team will always provide services with care and professionalism, following the Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (“RCVS”) guidelines.

Geography: We currently provide services in the Greater London area. If you request an appointment outside of our core service area, we may decline and refer you to another practice.

Availability: We will do our best to accommodate your preferred appointment time. Our availability is subject to the Veterinary Practitioner's schedule, traffic, and other variables, especially with our home visits.

Subscription Plans: As an alternative to purchasing one-off services and treatments (what we call “Pay As You Go” or “PAYG”), we also offer the “Pack Membership” in accordance with Part C of these Terms.

Veterinary Practitioners: Our Veterinary Surgeons and Nurses are all registered with the RCVS, ensuring that they meet high professional standards and adhere to continuing professional development (“CPD”) requirements. In short:

  • We recommend regular health checks for your pet, and we'll keep records in line with RCVS guidelines.
  • We will only prescribe treatment, including repeat prescriptions, if we have enough information about your pet's health to ensure it's the appropriate treatment.
  • If we feel that no treatment is necessary, we won't prescribe anything.
  • All treatments are carried out in line with current laws and regulations.
  • We'll discuss treatment plans with you beforehand and get your consent before taking any action. The only exception may be in an emergency, which is outlined further below.

2. Consultations

Confirmations: Once we accept your appointment request, we'll confirm all details via email or SMS. Please double-check that the information is accurate and get in touch if we need to correct anything.

Follow-up appointments: We'll do our best to book the same Veterinary Practitioner for any follow-up visits, but we can't always guarantee their availability.

Duration: Our standard appointments are booked for 60 minutes, while euthanasia and complex appointments are booked for at least 120 minutes. If your consultation runs longer than planned, we may adjust the fee accordingly. We'll always let you know before continuing beyond the planned time.

Costs: Pricing for the most common services and treatments are on our website. If further tests or investigations are needed, we'll inform you of the cost.

Treatments: It's your responsibility to follow up on any recommended actions, such as booking additional appointments or tests. While you are not obligated to proceed, if you choose not to, you agree to release us from any liability.

Sedation: In some situations, the Veterinary Practitioner may recommend that a pet be sedated before carrying out a service or treatment to protect safety and/or comfort.

  • Sedation is charged as a separate service, and we'll never sedate your pet without your consent.
  • If you do not consent to sedation, the Veterinary Practitioner may decline to provide the service if they don't feel they can proceed safely, at their sole discretion.
  • If the Veterinary Practitioner declines to provide the treatment without sedation, you'll still be charged for the call-out fee and any other treatments or services that are performed.

Virtual Consultations: We offer virtual care where you can connect with our Veterinary Practitioners through video, chat, or audio to help triage your pet's health issues remotely.

  • Virtual care isn't a substitute for physical visits. If your pet needs a hands-on check-up, we'll let you know.
  • Virtual care is not suitable for emergencies. If we think your pet needs urgent care, we'll urge you to go to your nearest emergency vet. Signs requiring immediate emergency care include: heavy bleeding, unresponsiveness, major injuries, suspected poisoning, heat stroke, swollen abdomen, continuous seizures, pregnancy complications, choking, trouble passing urine or stools, visible signs of distress or pain, refusing food or water for more than 24 hours, severe vomiting or diarrhoea, severe lameness or paralysis, and breathing problems.
  • Please ensure you're ready at your scheduled time. If you're unavailable or have technical issues, you may miss the appointment and we may still need to charge for the session.

3. Fees & Payments

  • New customers are eligible for a discounted first appointment. The terms of this are covered in Part B.
  • We charge a home visit or call-out fee for every in-person appointment, unless you have Membership in our subscription plan, covered in Part C.
  • Consultation fees vary depending on the type of consultation, its length, and the Veterinary Practitioner. Unless stated otherwise, the fee only covers time spent with the Practitioner. Additional medications or services will incur extra charges.
  • We publish pricing for the most common services and treatments on our website.
  • Fees are estimated based on our standard rates. While we do our best to be clear upfront, prices may vary based on factors like your pet's size, complexity, or unexpected circumstances.
  • All fees are quoted in pounds sterling and include VAT at the applicable rate.
  • Additional charges may apply for emergencies, out-of-hours care, or services outside our usual areas.
  • We accept Apple Pay, Google Pay, and all major debit and credit cards. Payments are due in full at the end of your appointment.
  • For certain services, we may ask for advance payment before we proceed.
  • If any payment remains unpaid, we may suspend services until the balance is settled. We may also charge interest based on the SONIA rate plus 3% per annum, plus an admin fee and any associated costs from third-party debt collection agencies.

4. Your Responsibilities

  • Ensure an adult is present: You must be at least 18 years old to use our services. Please ensure an adult is present during appointments.
  • Provide a safe environment: Our Practitioners reserve the right to stop a consultation if safety is compromised. You may still be charged for the call-out and any services already performed.
  • Provide accurate information: Please provide honest, complete details about your pet's health. We can't be responsible for advice given based on incomplete or incorrect information.
  • Payment of fees: Booking the appointment means you agree to pay in full at the time of treatment.
  • Follow aftercare instructions: Please follow any instructions regarding treatments, medications, or next steps. If you notice any unusual effects, let us know right away.

5. Medications

  • All medications are dispensed in line with the RCVS guidelines and the Veterinary Medicines Directorate regulations.
  • Medication will only be prescribed for pets currently under our care. You can request a prescription with a minimum of 48 hours' notice.
  • We will provide clear instructions with each prescription. All medication must be stored and administered as directed.
  • It's important not to alter any prescriptions without the approval of the prescribing veterinary surgeon.
  • It's against the law to sell on any veterinary medicines that have been dispensed to you.
  • Please dispose of unused or expired medication properly, or return it to us.

6. Repeat & Posted Prescriptions

  • We offer a repeat prescription service for pets on long-term medication.
  • Your pet must be seen by a vet at least once every six months.
  • We reserve the right to refuse to fill prescriptions in certain circumstances. For health or hygiene reasons, we cannot offer refunds for sealed medication.
  • Posted medication will be sent via recorded delivery, with an additional charge for postage and packing.

7. Consent

  • By agreeing to these Terms, you consent to our Practitioners following best practices regarding any treatment, including euthanasia, as deemed appropriate.
  • A verbal agreement during the consultation will be considered sufficient authorisation to proceed.
  • If we need to access your pet's medical records from another vet, we'll ask for your consent.

8. Referrals and Second Opinions

  • Sometimes it may be necessary to refer your pet to a specialist. We won't charge a referral fee, but the other vet may charge for their services.
  • Services performed by any third party are subject to their conditions.
  • If you want a second opinion, we're happy to help transfer relevant records.

9. Ownership of Clinic Records

Your pet's medical records belong to us, but you're welcome to request a copy to share with another practice or insurer. An administration fee may apply.

10. Cancellations

  • Some things outside our control (like weather or traffic) may cause delays. We'll update you in a timely manner.
  • If we cancel, you won't be charged.
  • If you need to cancel, please let us know at least 24 hours in advance. Cancellations less than 24 hours before the appointment may incur a charge of up to 100% of the consultation fee (unless you are cancelling within 14 days of booking, per your statutory rights).

11. Out of Hours and Emergencies

  • If we can't help directly, we'll refer you to a trusted partner.
  • There may be an additional charge for out-of-hours services.
  • For emergencies during out of hours, please call us on 03332 244863.

12. Zero Tolerance Policy

We have a strict zero-tolerance policy regarding abusive behaviour. We ask that you refrain from swearing or verbal abuse, threatening behaviour, intoxication, physical abuse, and inappropriate advances to our staff. If any of these occur, we may end your consultation immediately and reserve the right to stop providing services altogether.

13. Recording Appointments

We do not allow recording devices during consultations without express permission. If recording is found without permission, we may ask you to stop and delete any content, and we reserve the right to take legal action if recordings are shared publicly. Our team may take photos or videos during visits for promotional purposes, but only with your express consent.

14. Insurance

  • We highly recommend that you insure your pet.
  • We generally don't process direct insurance claims. All fees must be settled in full before we submit a claim on your behalf. An administration fee may apply.
  • We can't be held responsible if your insurer refuses your claim.
  • Some treatments may require pre-authorisation from insurers, and it's your responsibility to meet any submission deadlines.

15. Your Rights and Our Liability

  • We will be liable for foreseeable loss or damage you suffer as a result of our breach or negligence.
  • We are not liable for loss or damage that was not foreseeable, not caused by us, or any business loss.
  • We are not responsible for failure to perform caused by events outside our control (extreme weather, transport disruptions, power outages, etc.).
  • Treatment outcomes can't be guaranteed, and professional judgment will guide our care.

16. Data Privacy

  • We fully comply with GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018. We process your personal data in accordance with our Privacy Policy.
  • We collect only the necessary information to provide services, process payments, and contact you.
  • We might record chat messages, phone calls, and video consultations for training or monitoring purposes, but these won't be shared without your consent.
  • You have the right to access your personal data at any time.

17. Feedback, Questions and Complaints

  • If you have questions or need to raise a formal complaint, please email us at hello@tonysmobilevets.com or write to Customer Service, Tony's Mobile Vets Holdings Limited, 6 Kelvin Court, 24–26 Marlborough Road, Richmond, TW10 6JS.

  • These Terms supersede any previous agreements. If a court invalidates any part of this contract, the rest will still apply.

These Terms are governed by English law, and any disputes will be handled by the English courts unless you live in Wales, Scotland or Northern Ireland, in which case you can also bring claims in the courts of the country you live in. Part B

Your First Appointment (£99)

Your first visit from Tony's Mobile Vets costs just £99. Here's exactly what you get — and what to expect.

What's included

Your £99 first appointment covers two things:

  1. A call-out to your home. One of our RCVS-registered Veterinary Practitioners will visit you and your pet at a time that works for both of us — no stressful car journeys or waiting rooms.
  2. A full nose-to-tail health check and consultation. We'll carry out a thorough physical examination of your pet, from eyes and ears to coat, teeth, weight, heart, and joints. You'll also have time to raise any questions or concerns.

What's not included

The £99 covers your vet's visit and the health check consultation. Any treatments, medications, or additional services needed will be charged separately. For example: nail trims, vaccinations, sedation, prescribed medications, and diagnostic tests or lab work. Your vet will always discuss costs with you before proceeding.

Important: euthanasia appointments

The £99 first appointment offer is not applicable to euthanasia appointments. If you need to arrange end-of-life care, please contact us directly.

Multiple pets?

The £99 covers one pet. Additional pets during the same visit are just £50 each — each receives the same full health check and consultation.

Who can book?

The £99 first appointment is available to new customers and is limited to one per household, per pet. It applies within our Greater London service area. All fees include VAT at the applicable rate.

Part C

Pack Membership Terms & Conditions

These terms apply to the Pack Membership and should be read alongside our general Terms and Conditions.

1. What's included

The Pack Membership costs £39 per month per pet and includes the following at each of your included visits:

Home visit and consultation (included at every visit):

  • A call-out to your home by an RCVS-registered Veterinary Practitioner
  • A full nose-to-tail health check and consultation (up to 60 minutes)
  • Microchip scan

Complementary treatments:

  • Annual vaccination treatment
  • Nail clip, if required (PAYG price: £25)
  • Anal gland treatment, if required (PAYG price: £30)

Nail clips and anal gland treatments will only be carried out if clinically needed. If your pet requires sedation for any complementary treatment, sedation will be charged separately with your 15% member discount applied.

Visit allowance:

  • Up to 3 home visits per 12 calendar months, each including all of the above
  • Included visits can be used for vet consultations, nurse consultations, repeat consultations, priority consultations, and repeat prescription check-ups
  • Included visits cannot be used for euthanasia appointments, out-of-hours emergency visits, or surgical procedures — these are charged separately with your 15% member discount applied

15% member discount on everything else, including: additional visits beyond your 3 included, diagnostics, blood tests, lab work, sedation, medications, dental assessments, end-of-life care, cremation and keepsakes, travel health certificates, and all other treatments and add-on services.

3. Your first visit and the Pack Membership

If you sign up for a Pack Membership before your first appointment, your first visit will count as one of your 3 included appointments. The £99 first appointment fee will not apply.

If you have already had your £99 first appointment and then sign up, your 3 included appointments start fresh.

4. Multi-pet households

Additional pets from the same household can be added at £31.20 per month per pet (20% off the standard price). Each pet receives their own allowance of up to 3 visits per 12 calendar months with all the same included services. Each membership is exclusive to the registered pet and is not transferable.

5. Membership term and renewal

Your membership runs for an initial fixed term of 12 months. At the end of the term, it will automatically renew for a further 12 months at the price in effect at the time of renewal, unless you cancel. We will send reminder emails at least 60 days and 30 days before the end of each term.

6. Payment

You can pay by credit/debit card or direct debit. Payments are taken monthly. The direct debit payer must be named on the account. If we are unable to take payment after 2 attempts, we reserve the right to suspend or terminate your membership.

7. Prices

All prices are in pounds sterling and include VAT. We may change the price with at least 30 days' notice. If you do not wish to continue at the new price, you may cancel within that notice period.

8. Your right to cancel within 14 days

You may cancel within 14 days of your initial confirmation email for a full refund, less the cost of any treatments or services already received. If the value of treatments received exceeds the amount paid, you will be required to pay the difference.

9. Cancelling during your 12-month term

You must give at least 30 days' notice. You will be required to pay the full retail price of treatments and services received under the membership, or the outstanding amount due (whichever is lower), less the amount already paid. To cancel, email hello@tonysmobilevets.com or use the ‘Cancel Renewal’ button on your account.

10. Cancelling at the end of a term

Give at least 30 days' notice before your renewal date. Your membership will end at the conclusion of your current term and no further payments will be taken.

11. Cancellation due to pet bereavement

If your pet passes away during your membership term, we will cancel with no early termination charges. We will require reasonable evidence. Fees already paid for the current billing period will not be refunded, but no further payments will be taken. If you have multiple pets, only the membership for the deceased pet will be cancelled.

12. Our right to cancel

We reserve the right to cancel your membership with at least 30 days' written notice. If we cancel, you will be entitled to a pro-rata refund of any fees paid in advance for services not yet provided.

13. What the Pack Membership is not

The Pack Membership is not an insurance policy. It does not cover illnesses, accidents, hospitalisation, or emergency care. It covers preventative and routine care only. We strongly recommend that you also insure your pet separately.

14. Unused visits

Included visits do not roll over. Any unused visits will expire at the end of your 12-month period. No refund or credit will be given.

15. Auto-renewal transparency

When you sign up, we will clearly confirm: the monthly cost, the 12-month minimum term, that your membership will automatically renew, how to cancel, and your cancellation rights. This information will also be included in your confirmation email. We will send renewal reminders at 60 days and 30 days before each renewal date.
